Key Dishes

Key Dishes

Sushi (rice seasoned with vinegar, served with raw or cooked fish and vegetables)

Ramen (noodle soup with broth, meat and vegetables)

Characteristics

Emphasis on freshness, presentation and balance.
Use of soy sauce, miso, seaweed and rice as common ingredients.

Serving food beautifully is considered a sign of respect for the eater.

ðŸŸ£ 3. Indian Cuisine

Key Dishes

Curry (dishes cooked with rich, spiced sauces)

Chapati (flatbread made from wheat flour)

Biryani (spiced rice cooked with meat or vegetables)

Characteristics

Extensive use of spices such as turmeric, cumin, coriander and cardamom.
Regional variety: some areas prefer very spicy food, others milder flavours.
Many vegetarian dishes using lentils, beans and vegetables.

Cultural Significance

Food is often eaten with the hands, especially using flatbread to scoop curry.
Meals are shared with family and guests, showing hospitality and care.

Characteristics

Use of quick cooking methods like stir-frying in a hot wok.
Combination of sweet, sour, salty and spicy tastes in one meal.
Rice or noodles served as the main carbohydrate.

Cultural Significance

Dumplings are common during New Year celebrations as a sign of good luck.
Sharing many dishes in the middle of the table encourages togetherness.
